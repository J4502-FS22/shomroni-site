The Harry Potter Books: Worst to Best

As a major Harry Potter fan who's read the books countless times, I decided to rank them in order of my least favorite to favorite book.

Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets

By J.K. Rowling

Although it's my least favorite, I still enjoyed this book. It introduced the first horcrux, so I'll give it props for that. However, this wasn't enough to rank it any higher.

Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone

By J.K. Rowling

I didn't want to rank the first book last, because the introduction to the wizarding world is always fun to read. It only gets better as the series goes on, though.

Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix

By J.K. Rowling

Everyone seems to dislike this book because it's "boring." I disagree, but not enough to rank it any higher.

Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince

By J.K. Rowling

Things got real in this book, and I probably read it in one night. If asked on a different day, I might have ranked it even higher, who knows?

Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban

By J.K. Rowling

The book that introduces Sirius Black will always be in the top three.

Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire

By J.K. Rowling

This book was simply just exciting. The Quidditch World Cup, Triwizard Tournament and the return of Voldemort all in the same year? It's almost too much. Almost.

Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows

By J.K. Rowling

Maybe it's basic to rank the last book as the best one, but I don't care. It was really, really, really good.
